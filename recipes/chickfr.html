Garlic Chicken Fried Brown Rice

Description

Fried rice is a dish loved by many, especially in the western world. The dish's origin is relatively unknown, but it is believed to have been invented sometime during the Sui dynasty in Yangzhou, China.

As stated earlier, fried rice has become one of the west's favorites in Chinese cuisine. Today, you can find fried rice on the menus of practically every asian-based restaurant in the United States.

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil, divided
  • 8 ounces skinless, boneless chicken breast, cut into strips
  • 1/2 red bell pepper, chopped
  • 1/2 cup green onion, chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 3 cups cooked brown rice
  • 2 tablespoons light soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 cup frozen peas

Steps

  1. Heat 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil in a large skillet over medium heat.
  2. Add the chicken, bell pepper, green onion, and garlic.
  3. Cook and stir until the chicken is cooked through, about 5 minutes.
  4. Remove the chicken to a plate and keep warm.
  5. Heat the remaining tablespoon of oil in the same skillet over medium-high heat.
  6. Add the rice; cook and stir to heat through.
  7. Stir in the soy sauce, rice vinegar and peas, and continue to cook for 1 minute.
  8. Return the chicken mixture to the skillet and blend with the rice and heat through before serving.
